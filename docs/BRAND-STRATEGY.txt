## üì¢ Brand Voice & Tone

### Voice Characteristics

| Trait | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Knowledgeable** | Expert without being condescending | "Substrate flatness matters for tile sizes over 15 inches" |
| **Plain-Speaking** | Technical made accessible | "You don't need to speak 'construction' to hire well" |
| **Reassuring** | Confidence without arrogance | "We follow published methods‚Äînot guesswork" |
| **Direct** | Clear and actionable | "Written scope. Photo documentation. Warranty backed." |

### Tone Guidelines

**DO:**
- Use industry terms, then explain them
- Lead with the benefit to the homeowner
- Back claims with standards (TCNA, ANSI, HIC)
- Acknowledge the homeowner's perspective

**DON'T:**
- Use jargon without context
- Make promises without backing
- Dismiss competitors‚Äîlet work speak
- Oversell or use superlatives carelessly

### Writing Style

- **Sentence length:** Vary between short punchy sentences and longer explanatory ones
- **Active voice:** "We verify substrate flatness" not "Substrate flatness is verified"
- **Numbers:** Spell out one through nine, use numerals for 10+
- **Standards:** Always capitalize and format correctly: "TCNA 2024 Handbook"

## ‚úÖ Brand Checklist

### Before Publishing Content

- [ ] Does it align with brand pillars (Technical Authority, Transparency, Accountability, Craftsmanship)?
- [ ] Is the tone knowledgeable but accessible?
- [ ] Are technical claims backed by standards (TCNA, ANSI)?
