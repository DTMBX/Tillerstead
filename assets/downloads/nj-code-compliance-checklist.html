IRC 2018/2021 bathroom requirements for Atlantic County, NJ | Tillerstead LLC

Permit & Licensing (BEFORE Work Starts)

Building permit obtained: All shower/bathroom work in NJ requires permit. Apply at local construction office. Permit # visible on job site.
NJ HIC license verified: Contractor must be licensed for work >$500. Verify at njconsumeraffairs.gov. License # on contract.
Insurance certificates: General liability + workers comp. Call insurance company to verify active coverage.

Inspection #1: Rough-In (IRC Chapter 3 & 7)

Code Reference Requirement
IRC R307.1 Ventilation: 50 CFM exhaust fan OR window (‚â•3 sq ft, 50% operable). Fan vents to exterior (NOT attic).
IRC R302.5 Tub/shower enclosure: Walls must be smooth, non-absorbent to 6" above fixture or showerhead.
IRC P2708.1 Shower valve height: 38"-48" above finished floor (not subfloor). Account for mortar bed + tile thickness.
IRC P2709.2 Drain size: 2" minimum for shower drain. Proper trap (P-trap or built-in).
IRC P3105.1 Fixture clearances: 24" clear space in front of toilet, 21" minimum from centerline to wall/fixture.
IRC E3902.1 GFCI protection: All outlets within 6' of sink/shower. Arc-fault + GFCI combo breaker OK.

Inspection #2: Waterproofing (IRC R302.5.2 ‚Äî THE CRITICAL ONE)

Code Reference Requirement
IRC R302.5.2 Waterproofing REQUIRED: "Floors under shower compartments and receptors shall be waterproofed." Membrane must meet ANSI A118.10 performance standard.
ANSI A118.10 Membrane standard: Load-bearing waterproof membrane. Must pass 72-hour submersion test with zero leakage. Examples: Schluter Kerdi, Laticrete Hydroban, Wedi, Mapei Aquadefense.
IRC R307.2 Shower drainage: Floor slopes to drain (1/4" per foot minimum). Standing water prohibited.
Membrane installed per manufacturer specs: Follow Schluter/Laticrete/Wedi instructions exactly. Installation manual on site.
Factory pre-formed corners: Inside corners use Kerdi-Kereck, outside corners use Kerdi-Kers (or equivalent). NO field-cut corners.
Membrane height: Extends 6" ABOVE shower head (minimum 72" from floor). Covers all walls exposed to water spray.
Curb/threshold waterproofed: Membrane extends over curb and down 3-4" on outside face. No gaps.
Penetrations sealed: All valve stems, shower arm, drain flange sealed per membrane manufacturer specs.
Flood test performed: Fill shower pan 2" deep, wait 24-48 hours. ZERO leaks. Document with photos (date/time stamped).
‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL: Inspector MUST approve waterproofing BEFORE tile installation. Once tile is up, waterproofing cannot be verified. Failed inspection = tear out tile and start over.

During Tile Installation (TCNA Standards)

Proper thinset for tile size: Large format (‚â•15" any side) requires LFT/LHT rated thinset. Standard tile uses A118.1 or A118.4.
Movement joints (EJ171): Silicone caulk (NOT grout) at all changes of plane: floor-to-wall, wall-to-ceiling, inside corners.
Shower floor slope: Minimum 1/4" per foot slope to drain. Water must drain within 15 minutes of showering.
Grout joint size: 1/16" minimum (rectified tile), 1/8"-3/16" standard. Too narrow = cracking risk.

Inspection #3: Final (IRC R302 & R307)

Code Reference Inspector Checks
IRC R302.5 Wall surfaces smooth, non-absorbent, approved material. Tile properly adhered (tap test for hollow spots).
IRC R307.1 Ventilation fan operates (50 CFM minimum). Ducting secure, terminates outside (NOT attic).
IRC P2708.1 Fixtures installed per manufacturer specs. Valve height 38"-48" AFF. No leaks (water test).
IRC E3902.1 GFCI outlets functional (test button works). Proper cover plates installed.
IRC R307.2 Shower drains properly (no standing water). Doors/curtains contain spray.
Water test: Run shower 15 minutes, check ceiling/walls below for leaks. Test BEFORE contractor leaves site.
Ventilation test: Fan clears steam within 10 minutes. No moisture buildup on mirrors/walls after 20 minutes.
Grout sealed: Penetrating sealer applied (not topical). Ask contractor for product name and re-seal schedule.
Certificate of Occupancy: Inspector signs off permit. Get copy for your records. Required for insurance claims and resale.

County-Specific Notes (Atlantic, Ocean, Cape May)

County Special Requirements
Atlantic County Permit: Atlantic County Clerk (609-645-5898). 3 inspections required. Average approval: 7-10 business days. Flood zone work requires elevation certificate in coastal areas.
Ocean County Permit: Ocean County Clerk (732-929-2018). Stricter flood requirements (Zone A/V). Waterproofing inspection often separate from rough-in.
Cape May County Permit: Cape May County Planning (609-465-1037). Coastal building codes (high wind/flood). Additional structural review if altering load-bearing walls.

Common Violations & Penalties

No permit: $500-$2,000 fine + permit application + all inspections + possible tear-out. Voids insurance. Kills resale.
Failed waterproofing inspection: Must remove tile, fix membrane, re-inspect, re-tile. Cost: $4,000-$8,000+.
No GFCI protection: Immediate fail. Safety hazard (electrocution risk). $200-$500 to fix + re-inspection.
Inadequate ventilation: Moisture buildup = mold. Must install proper fan + ductwork. $600-$1,200 fix.
Work by unlicensed contractor: Homeowner liable for all corrections. No legal recourse against contractor. Insurance may deny claims.
