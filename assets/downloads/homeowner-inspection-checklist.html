Before Work Starts (Pre-Contract)

Contractor licensed? Ask for NJ HIC license number (verify at njconsumeraffairs.gov). License #13VH10808800 is valid. "I'm getting it soon" = run.
Insurance certificates? General liability + workers comp. Call the insurance company to verify it's active. Expired insurance = you're liable if someone gets hurt.
ANSI A118.10 waterproofing? Ask "What waterproofing membrane are you using and is it ANSI A118.10 compliant?" Correct answer: Schluter Kerdi, Laticrete Hydroban, or equivalent with certification.
Permit included? Any shower work in NJ requires a building permit. "Let's skip the permit" = code violations + voided insurance + impossible to sell your house.
Written contract? Must include: scope of work, timeline, payment schedule, materials list, permit details, contractor info. Handshake deal = disaster waiting to happen.

During Framing & Plumbing (Rough-In Inspection)

Walls framed correctly? 2x6 studs if using niches (2x4 walls can't fit recessed niches properly). 16" on center spacing. No "creative" framing.
Shower valve height? 38"-48" above finished floor (not subfloor‚Äîaccount for mortar bed + tile). Too high = awkward. Too low = kids can't reach.
Drain slope ready? Shower floor should slope 1/4" per foot toward drain. Use a level to check. Flat floor = standing water forever.
Inspection scheduled? Contractor must call for rough-in inspection BEFORE installing waterproofing or backer board. Skipping inspection = code violation.

Waterproofing Stage (Critical)

‚ö†Ô∏è RED FLAG ALERT: If your contractor says "we don't need waterproofing" or "the backer board is waterproof" ‚Äî STOP WORK IMMEDIATELY. This is the #1 cause of shower failures.
Membrane brand visible? Should see Schluter, Laticrete, Wedi, or equivalent packaging/labels on site. Mystery membrane = problem.
Factory corners used? Pre-formed Schluter Kerdi-Kers/Kerdi-Kereck or equivalent. NOT hand-cut membrane folded in corners. Corners are where leaks start.
Membrane height correct? Must extend 6" ABOVE shower head (not just to the shower head). Water splashes higher than you think.
Flood test performed? Fill shower pan with 2" of water, let sit 24-48 hours. ZERO leaks allowed. Take photos. Contractor should document this for the inspector.
Waterproofing inspection passed? Inspector must approve waterproofing BEFORE tile goes up. Once tile is installed, you can't verify what's behind it.

Tile Installation

Proper thinset? Large format tile (12x24+) requires large-and-heavy-tile (LHT) thinset. Regular thinset on large tile = future failures.
Thinset coverage? Should see 95%+ coverage when you pull a tile off (ask contractor to show you one). Hollow-sounding tiles = inadequate coverage.
Grout joints correct? 1/16" minimum for rectified tile, 1/8"-3/16" for standard tile. Too narrow = cracking. Too wide = ugly.
Movement joints present? Silicone (NOT grout) at all changes of plane: floor-to-wall, wall-to-ceiling, corners. Grout cracks. Silicone flexes.
Niche waterproofed? If you have a built-in niche, membrane must extend INTO and AROUND the niche. Common failure point if skipped.

Final Inspection & Completion

Final inspection passed? Building inspector must approve the completed work. Get a copy of the signed-off permit for your records.
Water test done? Run the shower for 15 minutes, check for leaks in ceiling/walls below. Do this before contractor leaves.
Ventilation working? 50 CFM exhaust fan minimum (required by IRC R307.1). Should clear steam within 10 minutes of showering.
Grout sealed? Grout should be sealed with penetrating sealer (not topical). Ask contractor what product they used and when to reseal.
Warranty documentation? Get warranty info for membrane system, tile, fixtures. Most manufacturers require registration within 30-90 days.
