Most “emergencies” aren’t surprises—they’re small problems that went unseen too long. A maintenance plan keeps you ahead of leaks, rot, loose fixtures, grout failures, caulk breakdown, and seasonal wear.

Good fit for: homeowners who want a reliable person to keep the property stable—without chasing contractors for every small fix.

How It Works

We start with a quick baseline visit, then move into a cadence that makes sense for your home.

  1. Baseline walk-through: we identify priority items, safety issues, and likely seasonal trouble spots.
  2. Written plan: you get a simple scope and visit cadence (monthly / quarterly / seasonal).
  3. Visits + documentation: each visit includes notes, photos, and clear “now vs later” recommendations.
  4. Small repairs handled: minor fixes are addressed on-site when possible; larger work is quoted separately.

What’s Typically Included

Inspection & Prevention

  • Visual checks for leaks, staining, moisture, and ventilation issues
  • Caulk and grout condition checks in wet areas
  • Door/trim/hinge issues, loose hardware, and minor finish failures
  • Seasonal trouble spots (wind-driven rain, coastal humidity, freeze/thaw)

Minor Repairs & Touch-Ups

  • Small patch/repair items when feasible during the visit
  • Re-caulking small sections (as needed)
  • Minor adjustments and re-fastening
  • Punch-list style fixes that keep your home from sliding backward

Exact inclusions depend on the plan you choose and the condition of the property. Everything is documented in writing.

What’s Not Included (So Expectations Stay Clean)

  • Emergency plumbing/electrical response: if there’s an active leak or hazard, contact the appropriate licensed trade first.
  • Major remodel scope: full renovations are quoted separately with a written proposal.
  • Specialty trade work: work requiring separate licensure is coordinated or referred as needed.
